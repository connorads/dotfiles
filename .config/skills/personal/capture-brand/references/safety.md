# Safety

The goal is an on-brand page, not impersonation.

## Allowed

- Extracting public brand evidence for design direction.
- Producing a neutral sample page, mock landing page, style tile, component
  theme, or internal prototype.
- Using user-provided official assets when the user indicates they own or are
  authorised to use the brand.
- Recording trademark and licence unknowns so the user can resolve them.

## Constrain

Constrain the deliverable when the request could imply affiliation or trust:

- Avoid login, signup, checkout, payment, account recovery, banking, wallet,
  healthcare, government, or credential screens.
- Avoid exact navigation, copy, pricing tables, plan names, legal text, and
  claims.
- Use placeholder content and neutral page framing.
- Add clear source and permission notes in `brand-brief.md`.

## Refuse or redirect

Do not help create:

- A deceptive clone intended to trick users.
- Credential or payment capture.
- Fake support, fake account, fake verification, or fake government/financial
  flows.
- Counterfeit product pages or unauthorised use designed to imply endorsement.
- A pixel-perfect reproduction of a protected current page when the user does
  not claim ownership or authorisation.

Offer a safe alternative: a style tile, brand-inspired neutral page, internal
prototype, or a kit that the user can review for rights.

## Licence and trademark handling

- Public availability does not mean reuse permission.
- Logos, marks, product images, and proprietary fonts may require permission.
- Third-party icon repositories can be stale. Prefer official source links and
  record uncertainty.
- Web-served fonts can be referenced by the original page, but bundling or
  redistributing font files needs a separate rights check.
